# Recruiter signals

A recruiter spends 6–10 seconds on the first pass. Make those seconds count.

## What the first-pass scanner looks for
1. Current/most-recent role title — does it look like the role they're hiring for?
2. Current/most-recent company — recognizable? Adjacent industry?
3. Years of experience — quick math from the dates.
4. Top 3–5 keywords from the JD — present in Skills and visible in the top role's bullets?
5. Location/work-auth signal — does the candidate match the role's geography?

If any of those five fail, the resume gets bucketed before bullets are read. Optimize the top third of page one for these.

## What the second-pass (hiring manager) reads
- Role progression: did this person get more senior, more scope, more ownership over time?
- Stack overlap: 60–80% of the JD's stack visible without strain?
- Domain overlap: relevant industry, relevant problem space?
- One or two bullets per role that prove the candidate has done the actual hard thing.

## Tailoring levers (in order of impact)
1. **Title alignment** — if the JD says "Staff Engineer" and the candidate's title is "Senior Engineer" doing staff-scope work, surface the scope in the role's first bullet. Do not falsify titles.
2. **Skills section reorder** — most-relevant stack first. Drop irrelevant skills entirely.
3. **Top-bullet reorder per role** — JD-relevant bullets sit first within each role.
4. **Summary line** — three lines, tailored. Skip it if it's just filler.
5. **Project selection** — keep 2–4 projects, all defensible, all relevant.

## Watch-outs by role archetype
- **Startup/early-stage**: emphasize end-to-end ownership, scrappiness, breadth, shipping speed.
- **Big tech / scale**: emphasize scope, system size, architectural rigor, cross-team collaboration.
- **Infra/platform**: emphasize reliability, observability, on-call ownership, multi-tenant concerns.
- **Applied AI / ML platform**: emphasize evals, inference paths, latency/cost tradeoffs, model + product loop.
- **Forward-deployed / solutions**: emphasize customer-facing scope, prototyping speed, communication.

## Red flags recruiters scan for
- Unexplained gaps > 6 months — address them honestly in a one-line note or cover letter, never in the resume body.
- Job hopping (< 12 months per role) without a story — group short contracts under one heading where honest.
- A skill list with 40 items — looks unfocused. Cap at ~15, all defensible.
- Buzzwords with no backing bullet ("scalable", "innovative", "passionate") — delete them.
